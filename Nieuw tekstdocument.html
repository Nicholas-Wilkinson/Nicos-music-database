HISTORY OF SPREACOMBE

Spreacombe was first mentioned in the Domesday Book. Through much of the Medieval and Early Modern period it was owned by the Arundell family. By the end of the eighteenth century it was in the hands of the Lucas family. The Riddells took it over in the early nineteenth century. It was Robert Andrew Riddell who built the current Manor House. The nineteenth-century census returns also provide information about the farmers and farm workers in the valley. At the end of the nineteenth century Spreacombe was briefly owned by Charles Symons. He sold it on to Major Style. In the 1920s it was sold to Lady Cecil and Arthur Barker. On Arthur Barker's death it was sold to Haywoods and in 1963 the current family, the Dennisses, took it over.
